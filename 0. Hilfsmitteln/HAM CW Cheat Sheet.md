## Common Ham Radio CW Abbreviation Cheat Sheet
> Last update: 24.04.26

###  Before calling / checking frequency
|       CW       | Meaning                      | Use it like this                                                     |
| :------------: | :--------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
|     *QRL?*     | Is this frequency busy?      | Before calling, send *QRL?* to check if the frequency is free.       |
|     *QRL*      | This frequency is busy.      | If someone sends *QRL*, move to another frequency.                   |
| *QRV?* / *QRV* | Are you ready? / I am ready. | Send *QRV?* to ask if the other station is ready. Answer with *QRV*. |
|      *AS*      | Wait / stand by.             | Send *AS* when you need the other station to wait.                   |

### Calling someone
|   CW    | Meaning                                      | Use it like this                                            |
| :-----: | :------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
|  *CQ*   | Calling any station.                         | Send *CQ* when you want anyone to answer.                   |
| *CQ DX* | Calling distant stations.                    | Send *CQ DX* if you mainly want long-distance contacts.     |
|  *DE*   | From / this is.                              | Use *DE* between the other call sign and your call sign.    |
|   *K*   | Go ahead, anyone may answer.                 | End a general call with *K*.                                |
|  *KN*   | Go ahead, only the named station may answer. | Use *KN* when you only want the station you named to reply. |
| *QRZ?*  | Who is calling me?                           | Send *QRZ?* if you hear a station but missed the call sign. |

### Greeting / first contact
|  CW   | Meaning                            | Use it like this                                         |
| :---: | :--------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| *GM*  | Good morning.                      | Use *GM* at the start of a morning contact.              |
| *GA*  | Good afternoon.                    | Use *GA* at the start of an afternoon contact.           |
| *GE*  | Good evening.                      | Use *GE* at the start of an evening contact.             |
| *DR*  | Dear.                              | Use *DR* before the operator’s name or friendly title.   |
| *OM*  | Friendly term for a male operator. | Use *OM* like “old man” in a friendly ham-radio way.     |
| *YL*  | Female operator.                   | Use *YL* when referring to a female operator.            |
| *TNX* | Thanks.                            | Use *TNX* to thank someone for calling or for the QSO.   |
| *PSE* | Please.                            | Use *PSE* when asking the other station to do something. |

### Signal report / copy quality
|       CW       | Meaning                             | Use it like this                                                    |
| :------------: | :---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
|     *RST*      | Readability, strength, tone report. | Give a signal report with *RST*, for example *RST* 579.             |
| *599* / *5NN*  | Perfect CW signal report.           | Use *5NN* instead of *599* in contests or fast contacts.            |
|     *HW?*      | How do you copy me?                 | After giving your report, send *HW?* to ask how you are received.   |
| *CPY?* / *CPY* | Did you copy? / I copied.           | Ask with *CPY?* if you are unsure whether the message was received. |
|      *R*       | Roger / received.                   | Send *R* to show that you received the information.                 |
| *QSL?* / *QSL* | Did you receive? / I confirm.       | Ask with *QSL?* and answer with *QSL*.                              |
|     *RPT*      | Repeat.                             | Send *RPT* when you need something sent again.                      |
|     *AGN*      | Again.                              | Use *AGN* when asking the other station to send something again.    |

### Name / operator
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*OP?* / *OP*|What is your operator name? / Operator name is.|Ask with *OP?* and answer with *OP* followed by your name.|
|*NAME?* / *NAME*|What is your name? / Name is.|Use *NAME?* if you want the other operator’s name.|
|*QRA?* / *QRA*|What is the name or call sign of your station? / Station name is.|Use *QRA?* when asking for station identification.|

### Location
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*QTH?* / *QTH*|What is your location? / My location is.|Ask with *QTH?* and answer with *QTH* followed by your city or location.|
|*LOC?* / *LOC*|What is your grid locator? / My locator is.|Ask with *LOC?* and answer with *LOC* followed by your Maidenhead locator.|

### Time / weather
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*QTR?* / *QTR*|What is the correct time? / The time is.|Ask with *QTR?* and answer with *QTR* followed by the time.|
|*UTC* / *Z*|Coordinated Universal Time / Zulu time.|Use *UTC* or *Z* when giving radio time.|
|*WX?* / *WX*|What is the weather? / Weather is.|Ask with *WX?* and answer with *WX* followed by the weather.|
|*TEMP?* / *TEMP*|What is the temperature? / Temperature is.|Ask with *TEMP?* and answer with *TEMP* followed by the temperature.|

### Signal problems
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*QRM?* / *QRM*|Is there interference? / There is interference.|Use *QRM* when other stations or signals are interfering.|
|*QRN?* / *QRN*|Is there static or natural noise? / There is static or noise.|Use *QRN* when lightning or band noise makes copying hard.|
|*QSB?* / *QSB*|Are my signals fading? / Your signals are fading.|Use *QSB* when the signal gets stronger and weaker.|
|*QRH?* / *QRH*|Is my frequency drifting? / Your frequency is drifting.|Use *QRH* if a station is not staying on frequency.|
|*QSD?* / *QSD*|Is my keying defective? / Your keying is defective.|Use *QSD* if the Morse sending is badly formed.|

### Speed and sending changes
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*QRS?* / *QRS*|Should I send slower? / Send slower.|Send *QRS* if the other station is too fast.|
|*QRQ?* / *QRQ*|Should I send faster? / Send faster.|Send *QRQ* if you want the other station to speed up.|
|*WPM*|Words per minute.|Use *WPM* when talking about Morse speed.|
|*QRT?* / *QRT*|Should I stop sending? / Stop sending or going off air.|Use *QRT* when you are ending operation or telling someone to stop.|
|*QRX?* / *QRX*|When will you call again? / Wait or call again later.|Send *QRX* when you need the other station to wait.|

### Frequency changes
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*QRG?* / *QRG*|What is my exact frequency? / Your frequency is.|Ask with *QRG?* when you want a frequency check.|
|*QSY?* / *QSY*|Should I change frequency? / Change frequency.|Use *QSY* when moving to another frequency.|
|*QSX?* / *QSX*|Will you listen on another frequency? / I am listening on another frequency.|Use *QSX* for split-frequency operation.|

### Station equipment
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*RIG?* / *RIG*|What radio are you using? / Radio is.|Ask with *RIG?* and answer with *RIG* followed by the radio model.|
|*ANT?* / *ANT*|What antenna are you using? / Antenna is.|Ask with *ANT?* and answer with *ANT* followed by the antenna type.|
|*PWR?* / *PWR*|What power are you using? / Power is.|Ask with *PWR?* and answer with *PWR* followed by watts.|
|*QRP*|Low power.|Use *QRP* when operating with low power, usually 5 watts or less on CW.|
|*QRO*|High power.|Use *QRO* when talking about higher transmit power.|
|*RX*|Receiver.|Use *RX* when talking about the receiving side.|
|*TX*|Transmitter / transmit.|Use *TX* when talking about the transmitting side.|
|*SWR*|Standing wave ratio.|Use *SWR* when discussing antenna matching.|

### DX / propagation
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*DX*|Long-distance contact.|Use *DX* when calling or talking about distant stations.|
|*QRB?* / *QRB*|What is the distance between us? / Distance is.|Ask with *QRB?* if you want to know the distance.|
|*COND?* / *COND*|What are the band conditions? / Conditions are.|Use *COND* when describing propagation.|
|*LP*|Long path.|Use *LP* when the signal travels the long way around Earth.|
|*SP*|Short path.|Use *SP* when the signal travels the shorter way around Earth.|

### Messages / traffic
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*QTC?* / *QTC*|Do you have a message? / I have a message.|Ask with *QTC?* when checking for traffic.|
|*QSP?* / *QSP*|Will you relay? / I will relay.|Use *QSP* when passing a message through another station.|
|*MSG*|Message.|Use *MSG* when referring to a message.|
|*NR*|Number.|Use *NR* when giving a message or contact number.|
|*AA*|All after.|Use *AA* when asking to repeat everything after a certain word.|
|*AB*|All before.|Use *AB* when asking to repeat everything before a certain word.|
|*BN*|All between.|Use *BN* when asking to repeat everything between two words.|

### QSL / logging
|       CW       | Meaning                       | Use it like this                                        |
| :------------: | :---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
|   *QSL CARD*   | Confirmation card.            | Use *QSL CARD* when asking about paper confirmation.    |
|     *BURO*     | QSL bureau.                   | Use *BURO* when sending QSL cards through the bureau.   |
|     *VIA*      | Via / through.                | Use *VIA* before the route for a QSL card.              |
|     *LOG*      | Logbook.                      | Use *LOG* when confirming the contact is recorded.      |

### Closing / goodbye
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*CUL*|See you later.|Use *CUL* near the end of a friendly contact.|
|*CUAGN*|See you again.|Use *CUAGN* when you hope to contact the station again.|
|*73*|Best regards.|End most ham contacts with *73*.|
|*72*|Best regards, usually between low-power operators.|Use *72* instead of *73* when both stations are using low power.|
|*88*|Love and kisses.|Use *88* only when appropriate, often to someone you know well.|
|*CL*|Closing station.|Use *CL* when you are leaving the frequency.|
|*SK*|Final end of contact.|Use *SK* as the final sign-off.|

### Simple QSO flow
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*QRL?*|Is the frequency busy?|Send *QRL?* before calling.|
|*CQ*|Calling any station.|Send *CQ* to look for a contact.|
|*DE*|This is.|Use *DE* before your call sign.|
|*K*|Go ahead.|End your call with *K*.|
|*RST*|Signal report.|Give the other station an *RST* report.|
|*OP*|Operator name.|Send *OP* followed by your name.|
|*QTH*|Location.|Send *QTH* followed by your location.|
|*RIG*|Radio.|Send *RIG* followed by your radio model.|
|*ANT*|Antenna.|Send *ANT* followed by your antenna type.|
|*PWR*|Power.|Send *PWR* followed by your power in watts.|
|*WX*|Weather.|Send *WX* followed by the weather.|
|*QSL*|Confirmed.|Send *QSL* when you copied the information.|
|*TNX*|Thanks.|Send *TNX* before closing.|
|*73*|Best regards.|Send *73* at the end.|
|*SK*|End of contact.|Send *SK* as the final end.|
