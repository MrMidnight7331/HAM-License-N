## Common Ham Radio CW Abbreviation Cheat Sheet
> Last update: 24.04.26

###  Before calling / checking frequency
|       CW       | Meaning                                                       | Use it like this                                                                |
| :------------: | :------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _QRL?_ / _QRL_ | Is this frequency busy? / This frequency is busy.             | Send _QRL?_ before calling. If someone answers _QRL_, choose another frequency. |
| _QRV?_ / _QRV_ | Are you ready? / I am ready.                                  | Send _QRV?_ before starting. Answer with _QRV_.                                 |
|      _AS_      | Wait / stand by.                                              | Send _AS_ when you need the other station to wait.                              |
|      _BK_      | Break / pause transmission / back to you.                     | Send _BK_ when you pause and let the other station answer.                      |
|      _K_       | Invitation to transmit; anyone may answer.                    | End a general call with _K_.                                                    |
|      _KN_      | Invitation to transmit; only the named station should answer. | Use _KN_ when you only want the station you named to reply.                     |
|      _AR_      | End of transmission or end of message.                        | Send _AR_ after finishing a message, but before the final goodbye.              |
|      _SK_      | End of contact / final sign-off.                              | Send _SK_ as the final end of the contact.                                      |
|      _CL_      | Closing station.                                              | Send _CL_ when you are leaving the frequency.                                   |

### Calling someone
|    CW    | Meaning                            | Use it like this                                                     |
| :------: | :--------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
|   _CQ_   | Calling any station.               | Send _CQ_ when you want any station to answer.                       |
| _CQ DX_  | Calling distant stations.          | Send _CQ DX_ when you mainly want long-distance contacts.            |
| _CP ..._ | Calling several specific stations. | Send _CP_ followed by the call signs of several stations.            |
| _CS ..._ | Calling a specific station.        | Send _CS_ followed by the call sign of the station you want.         |
|  _CS?_   | What call sign?                    | Send _CS?_ if you need the other station’s call sign.                |
| _DE ..._ | From / this is.                    | Use _DE_ between the other station’s call sign and your call sign.   |
| _FM ..._ | From.                              | Use _FM_ when giving where a message or station is from.             |
|  _QRZ?_  | Who is calling me?                 | Send _QRZ?_ when you hear a call but did not copy it.                |
|  _LID_   | Poor operator; derogatory.         | Avoid sending _LID_ unless you intentionally want to insult someone. |

### Greeting / operator identity
|       CW       | Meaning                                                                    | Use it like this                                           |
| :------------: | :------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
|      _GM_      | Good morning.                                                              | Send _GM_ at the start of a morning contact.               |
|      _GA_      | Good afternoon.                                                            | Send _GA_ at the start of an afternoon contact.            |
|      _GE_      | Good evening.                                                              | Send _GE_ at the start of an evening contact.              |
|      _DR_      | Dear.                                                                      | Use _DR_ before a name or friendly title.                  |
|      _OM_      | Male radio operator; friendly ham term.                                    | Use _OM_ when greeting a male operator.                    |
|      _YL_      | Female radio operator.                                                     | Use _YL_ when referring to a female operator.              |
|     _XYL_      | Wife of a radio operator.                                                  | Use _XYL_ when referring to an operator’s wife.            |
|  _OP?_ / _OP_  | What is your operator name? / My operator name is.                         | Ask with _OP?_ and answer with _OP_ followed by your name. |
| _QRA?_ / _QRA_ | What is your station name or call sign? / My station name or call sign is. | Use _QRA?_ when asking for station identification.         |

### Signal report / copy quality
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|_RST_|Readability, signal strength, tone.|Give the other station an _RST_ report, followed by the numbers.|
|_599_ / _5NN_|Perfect CW signal report.|Use _5NN_ instead of _599_ in fast contacts or contests.|
|_RSN_|Readability, strength, noise. Rare alternative report style.|Use _RSN_ only if the other operator understands that report system.|
|_HW?_|How do you copy me?|Send _HW?_ after giving your report.|
|_CPY?_ / _CPY_|Did you copy? / I copied.|Ask with _CPY?_ if you are not sure the message was received.|
|_R_|Received as transmitted.|Send _R_ when you copied the information correctly.|
|_C_|Yes / correct / affirmative.|Send _C_ to confirm that something is correct.|
|_N_|No. Also sometimes used for the number nine in shortened reports.|Send _N_ to answer no, or as part of shortened contest-style numbers.|
|_OK_|Okay.|Send _OK_ to show that the information is understood.|
|_QSL?_ / _QSL_|Did you receive or confirm? / I confirm.|Ask with _QSL?_ and answer with _QSL_.|
|_CFM_|Confirm.|Send _CFM_ when you want confirmation.|
|_NIL_|I have nothing to send / nothing copied.|Send _NIL_ if you have no information or copied nothing.|

### Asking for repeats / fixing copy
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|_AGN_|Again.|Send _AGN_ when you want something repeated.|
|_RPT_|Repeat / report / I repeat as follows.|Send _RPT_ when asking the other station to send something again.|
|_I I_|I repeat / I say again.|Send _I I_ before repeating the same information.|
|_AA ..._|All after.|After a question mark, send _AA_ followed by the word after which you need repetition.|
|_AB ..._|All before.|After a question mark, send _AB_ followed by the word before which you need repetition.|
|_BN ... ..._|All between.|Send _BN_ with two reference words when you need the part between them repeated.|
|_WA ..._|Word after.|Send _WA_ followed by a word when you need the next word repeated.|
|_WB ..._|Word before.|Send _WB_ followed by a word when you need the previous word repeated.|
|_W_ / _WD_|Word / words.|Use _W_ or _WD_ when referring to a word in a message.|
|_CK_|Check.|Use _CK_ when checking the number of words or message accuracy.|
|_SFR_|So far.|Use _SFR_ when confirming only the part received so far.|

### Location / address / time / weather
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|_QTH?_ / _QTH_|What is your location? / My location is.|Ask with _QTH?_ and answer with _QTH_ followed by your location.|
|_LOC?_ / _LOC_|What is your grid locator? / My grid locator is.|Ask with _LOC?_ and answer with _LOC_ followed by your locator.|
|_ADRS_ / _ADS_|Address.|Use _ADRS_ or _ADS_ when giving or asking about an address.|
|_QTR?_ / _QTR_|What is the correct time? / The time is.|Ask with _QTR?_ and answer with _QTR_ followed by the time.|
|_Z ..._|Zulu time / Coordinated Universal Time.|Use _Z_ after a time to show it is Coordinated Universal Time.|
|_WX ..._|Weather / weather report follows.|Use _WX_ before giving the weather.|

|:-:|:--|---|

### Frequency / speed / operating changes
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|_QRG?_ / _QRG_|What is my exact frequency? / Your frequency is.|Ask with _QRG?_ when you want a frequency check.|
|_QSY?_ / _QSY_|Should I change frequency? / Change frequency.|Use _QSY_ when moving to another frequency.|
|_QSX?_ / _QSX_|Will you listen on another frequency? / I am listening on another frequency.|Use _QSX_ for split-frequency operation.|
|_QRS?_ / _QRS_|Should I send slower? / Send slower.|Send _QRS_ if the other station is too fast.|
|_QRQ?_ / _QRQ_|Should I send faster? / Send faster.|Send _QRQ_ if you want faster sending.|
|_QRX?_ / _QRX_|When will you call again? / Wait or call again later.|Send _QRX_ when you need the other station to wait.|
|_QRT?_ / _QRT_|Should I stop sending? / Stop sending or going off air.|Use _QRT_ when ending operation or telling a station to stop.|

### Signal problems
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|_DX_|Long-distance or foreign station.|Use _DX_ when talking about distant contacts.|
|_QRB?_ / _QRB_|What is the distance between us? / The distance is.|Ask with _QRB?_ if you want to know the distance.|
|_COND?_ / _COND_|What are the conditions? / Conditions are.|Use _COND_ when talking about propagation.|
|_LP_|Long path.|Use _LP_ when the signal travels the long way around Earth.|

### Messages / traffic handling
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|_MSG_|Message. Historically also a ship-operation message prefix.|Use _MSG_ when referring to a message.|
|_QTC?_ / _QTC_|Do you have traffic? / I have traffic.|Ask with _QTC?_ when checking for messages.|
|_QSP?_ / _QSP_|Will you relay? / I will relay.|Use _QSP_ when passing a message through another station.|
|_NR ... ..._|Number follows.|Use _NR_ before a message number or contact number.|
|_FWD_|Forward.|Use _FWD_ when a message should be sent onward.|
|_PX_|Prefix.|Use _PX_ when referring to the prefix part of a call sign.|
|_SIG_|Signature.|Use _SIG_ before a signature in formal traffic.|
|_PPR_|Paper.|Use _PPR_ in formal traffic when referring to paper.|
|_WC_|Will comply.|Send _WC_ to say you will do what was requested.|

### Politeness / simple answers
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|_PSE_|Please.|Use _PSE_ when asking for something.|
|_PLS_|Please.|Use _PLS_ like _PSE_, but do not send it after _CQ_.|
|_SVP_|Please, from French. Rare in normal ham contacts.|Use _SVP_ only if the other operator will understand it.|
|_TU_|Thank you.|Send _TU_ after receiving useful information.|
|_THX_|Thanks / thank you.|Send _THX_ in a friendly contact.|
|_FB_|Good / excellent. Literally “fine business.”|Send _FB_ when copy, signal, or information is good.|
|_ES_|And.|Use _ES_ to connect two pieces of information.|

### Closing / goodbye
|   CW    | Meaning                                                                                  | Use it like this                                                   |
| :-----: | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
|  _CUL_  | See you later.                                                                           | Use _CUL_ near the end of a friendly contact.                      |
| _CUAGN_ | See you again.                                                                           | Use _CUAGN_ when you hope to contact the station again.            |
|  _72_   | Best regards, usually for low-power operation.                                           | Send _72_ instead of _73_ when low-power operation is the focus.   |
|  _73_   | Best regards.                                                                            | Send _73_ at the end of most amateur radio contacts.               |
|  _88_   | Love and kisses.                                                                         | Use _88_ only when appropriate.                                    |
|  _161_  | Best regards plus love and kisses. Rare, often associated with female-operator networks. | Use _161_ only in the right social context.                        |
|  _77_   | Long live CW / wishing many good CW contacts.                                            | Use _77_ as a CW-friendly sign-off.                                |
|  _SK_   | Silent Key; a deceased radio amateur.                                                    | Outside a contact, _SK_ can refer to a radio amateur who has died. |

### Simple QSO flow
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*QRL?*|Is the frequency busy?|Send *QRL?* before calling.|
|*CQ*|Calling any station.|Send *CQ* to look for a contact.|
|*DE*|This is.|Use *DE* before your call sign.|
|*K*|Go ahead.|End your call with *K*.|
|*RST*|Signal report.|Give the other station an *RST* report.|
|*OP*|Operator name.|Send *OP* followed by your name.|
|*QTH*|Location.|Send *QTH* followed by your location.|
|*RIG*|Radio.|Send *RIG* followed by your radio model.|
|*ANT*|Antenna.|Send *ANT* followed by your antenna type.|
|*PWR*|Power.|Send *PWR* followed by your power in watts.|
|*WX*|Weather.|Send *WX* followed by the weather.|
|*QSL*|Confirmed.|Send *QSL* when you copied the information.|
|*TNX*|Thanks.|Send *TNX* before closing.|
|*73*|Best regards.|Send *73* at the end.|
|*SK*|End of contact.|Send *SK* as the final end.|
