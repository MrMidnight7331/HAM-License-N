## Common Ham Radio CW Abbreviation Cheat Sheet
> Last update: 24.04.26

###  Before calling / checking frequency
|       CW       | Meaning                                                       | Use it like this                                                                |
| :------------: | :------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| *QRL?* / *QRL* | Is this frequency busy? / This frequency is busy.             | Send *QRL?* before calling. If someone answers *QRL*, choose another frequency. |
| *QRV?* / *QRV* | Are you ready? / I am ready.                                  | Send *QRV?* before starting. Answer with *QRV*.                                 |
|      *AS*      | Wait / stand by.                                              | Send *AS* when you need the other station to wait.                              |
|      *BK*      | Break / pause transmission / back to you.                     | Send *BK* when you pause and let the other station answer.                      |
|      *K*       | Invitation to transmit; anyone may answer.                    | End a general call with *K*.                                                    |
|      *KN*      | Invitation to transmit; only the named station should answer. | Use *KN* when you only want the station you named to reply.                     |
|      *AR*      | End of transmission or end of message.                        | Send *AR* after finishing a message, but before the final goodbye.              |
|      *SK*      | End of contact / final sign-off.                              | Send *SK* as the final end of the contact.                                      |
|      *CL*      | Closing station.                                              | Send *CL* when you are leaving the frequency.                                   |

### Calling someone
|    CW    | Meaning                            | Use it like this                                                     |
| :------: | :--------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
|   *CQ*   | Calling any station.               | Send *CQ* when you want any station to answer.                       |
| *CQ DX*  | Calling distant stations.          | Send *CQ DX* when you mainly want long-distance contacts.            |
| *CP ...* | Calling several specific stations. | Send *CP* followed by the call signs of several stations.            |
| *CS ...* | Calling a specific station.        | Send *CS* followed by the call sign of the station you want.         |
|  *CS?*   | What call sign?                    | Send *CS?* if you need the other station’s call sign.                |
| *DE ...* | From / this is.                    | Use *DE* between the other station’s call sign and your call sign.   |
| *FM ...* | From.                              | Use *FM* when giving where a message or station is from.             |
|  *QRZ?*  | Who is calling me?                 | Send *QRZ?* when you hear a call but did not copy it.                |
|  *LID*   | Poor operator; derogatory.         | Avoid sending *LID* unless you intentionally want to insult someone. |

### Greeting / operator identity
|       CW       | Meaning                                                                    | Use it like this                                           |
| :------------: | :------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
|      *GM*      | Good morning.                                                              | Send *GM* at the start of a morning contact.               |
|      *GA*      | Good afternoon.                                                            | Send *GA* at the start of an afternoon contact.            |
|      *GE*      | Good evening.                                                              | Send *GE* at the start of an evening contact.              |
|      *DR*      | Dear.                                                                      | Use *DR* before a name or friendly title.                  |
|      *OM*      | Male radio operator; friendly ham term.                                    | Use *OM* when greeting a male operator.                    |
|      *YL*      | Female radio operator.                                                     | Use *YL* when referring to a female operator.              |
|     *XYL*      | Wife of a radio operator.                                                  | Use *XYL* when referring to an operator’s wife.            |
|  *OP?* / *OP*  | What is your operator name? / My operator name is.                         | Ask with *OP?* and answer with *OP* followed by your name. |
| *QRA?* / *QRA* | What is your station name or call sign? / My station name or call sign is. | Use *QRA?* when asking for station identification.         |

### Signal report / copy quality
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*RST*|Readability, signal strength, tone.|Give the other station an *RST* report, followed by the numbers.|
|*599* / *5NN*|Perfect CW signal report.|Use *5NN* instead of *599* in fast contacts or contests.|
|*RSN*|Readability, strength, noise. Rare alternative report style.|Use *RSN* only if the other operator understands that report system.|
|*HW?*|How do you copy me?|Send *HW?* after giving your report.|
|*CPY?* / *CPY*|Did you copy? / I copied.|Ask with *CPY?* if you are not sure the message was received.|
|*R*|Received as transmitted.|Send *R* when you copied the information correctly.|
|*C*|Yes / correct / affirmative.|Send *C* to confirm that something is correct.|
|*N*|No. Also sometimes used for the number nine in shortened reports.|Send *N* to answer no, or as part of shortened contest-style numbers.|
|*OK*|Okay.|Send *OK* to show that the information is understood.|
|*QSL?* / *QSL*|Did you receive or confirm? / I confirm.|Ask with *QSL?* and answer with *QSL*.|
|*CFM*|Confirm.|Send *CFM* when you want confirmation.|
|*NIL*|I have nothing to send / nothing copied.|Send *NIL* if you have no information or copied nothing.|

### Asking for repeats / fixing copy
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*AGN*|Again.|Send *AGN* when you want something repeated.|
|*RPT*|Repeat / report / I repeat as follows.|Send *RPT* when asking the other station to send something again.|
|*I I*|I repeat / I say again.|Send *I I* before repeating the same information.|
|*AA ...*|All after.|After a question mark, send *AA* followed by the word after which you need repetition.|
|*AB ...*|All before.|After a question mark, send *AB* followed by the word before which you need repetition.|
|*BN ... ...*|All between.|Send *BN* with two reference words when you need the part between them repeated.|
|*WA ...*|Word after.|Send *WA* followed by a word when you need the next word repeated.|
|*WB ...*|Word before.|Send *WB* followed by a word when you need the previous word repeated.|
|*W* / *WD*|Word / words.|Use *W* or *WD* when referring to a word in a message.|
|*CK*|Check.|Use *CK* when checking the number of words or message accuracy.|
|*SFR*|So far.|Use *SFR* when confirming only the part received so far.|

### Location / address / time / weather
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*QTH?* / *QTH*|What is your location? / My location is.|Ask with *QTH?* and answer with *QTH* followed by your location.|
|*LOC?* / *LOC*|What is your grid locator? / My grid locator is.|Ask with *LOC?* and answer with *LOC* followed by your locator.|
|*ADRS* / *ADS*|Address.|Use *ADRS* or *ADS* when giving or asking about an address.|
|*QTR?* / *QTR*|What is the correct time? / The time is.|Ask with *QTR?* and answer with *QTR* followed by the time.|
|*Z ...*|Zulu time / Coordinated Universal Time.|Use *Z* after a time to show it is Coordinated Universal Time.|
|*WX ...*|Weather / weather report follows.|Use *WX* before giving the weather.|

### Station equipment / radio setup
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*ANT*|Antenna.|Send *ANT* followed by your antenna type.|
|*RIG*|Radio setup.|Send *RIG* followed by your radio model.|
|*RX*|Receiver / receive.|Use *RX* when talking about the receiving side.|
|*TX*|Transmitter / transmit.|Use *TX* when talking about the transmitting side.|
|*XCVR*|Transceiver.|Use *XCVR* when your radio both transmits and receives.|
|*PWR*|Power.|Send *PWR* followed by your power in watts.|
|*QRP*|Low power operation.|Use *QRP* when operating with low power.|
|*QRO*|High power operation.|Use *QRO* when talking about higher transmit power.|

### Frequency / speed / operating changes
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*QRG?* / *QRG*|What is my exact frequency? / Your frequency is.|Ask with *QRG?* when you want a frequency check.|
|*QSY?* / *QSY*|Should I change frequency? / Change frequency.|Use *QSY* when moving to another frequency.|
|*QSX?* / *QSX*|Will you listen on another frequency? / I am listening on another frequency.|Use *QSX* for split-frequency operation.|
|*QRS?* / *QRS*|Should I send slower? / Send slower.|Send *QRS* if the other station is too fast.|
|*QRQ?* / *QRQ*|Should I send faster? / Send faster.|Send *QRQ* if you want faster sending.|
|*QRX?* / *QRX*|When will you call again? / Wait or call again later.|Send *QRX* when you need the other station to wait.|
|*QRT?* / *QRT*|Should I stop sending? / Stop sending or going off air.|Use *QRT* when ending operation or telling a station to stop.|

### Signal problems
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*DX*|Long-distance or foreign station.|Use *DX* when talking about distant contacts.|
|*QRB?* / *QRB*|What is the distance between us? / The distance is.|Ask with *QRB?* if you want to know the distance.|
|*COND?* / *COND*|What are the conditions? / Conditions are.|Use *COND* when talking about propagation.|
|*LP*|Long path.|Use *LP* when the signal travels the long way around Earth.|

### Messages / traffic handling
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*MSG*|Message. Historically also a ship-operation message prefix.|Use *MSG* when referring to a message.|
|*QTC?* / *QTC*|Do you have traffic? / I have traffic.|Ask with *QTC?* when checking for messages.|
|*QSP?* / *QSP*|Will you relay? / I will relay.|Use *QSP* when passing a message through another station.|
|*NR ... ...*|Number follows.|Use *NR* before a message number or contact number.|
|*FWD*|Forward.|Use *FWD* when a message should be sent onward.|
|*PX*|Prefix.|Use *PX* when referring to the prefix part of a call sign.|
|*SIG*|Signature.|Use *SIG* before a signature in formal traffic.|
|*PPR*|Paper.|Use *PPR* in formal traffic when referring to paper.|
|*WC*|Will comply.|Send *WC* to say you will do what was requested.|

### Politeness / simple answers
|  CW   | Meaning                                           | Use it like this                                         |
| :---: | :------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| *PSE* | Please.                                           | Use *PSE* when asking for something.                     |
| *PLS* | Please.                                           | Use *PLS* like *PSE*, but do not send it after *CQ*.     |
| *SVP* | Please, from French. Rare in normal ham contacts. | Use *SVP* only if the other operator will understand it. |
| *TU*  | Thank you.                                        | Send *TU* after receiving useful information.            |
| *THX* | Thanks / thank you.                               | Send *THX* in a friendly contact.                        |
| *FB*  | Good / excellent. Literally “fine business.”      | Send *FB* when copy, signal, or information is good.     |
| *ES*  | And.                                              | Use *ES* to connect two pieces of information.           |

### Closing / goodbye
|   CW    | Meaning                                                                                  | Use it like this                                                             |
| :-----: | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
|  *CUL*  | See you later.                                                                           | Use *CUL* near the end of a friendly contact.                                |
| *CUAGN* | See you again.                                                                           | Use *CUAGN* when you hope to contact the station again.                      |
|  *72*   | Best regards, usually for low-power operation.                                           | Send *72* instead of *73* when low-power operation is the focus.             |
|  *73*   | Best regards.                                                                            | Send *73* at the end of most amateur radio contacts.                         |
|  *88*   | Love and kisses.                                                                         | Use *88* only when appropriate.                                              |
|  *161*  | Best regards plus love and kisses. Rare, often associated with female-operator networks. | Use *161* only in the right social context.                                  |
|  *77*   | Long live CW / wishing many good CW contacts.                                            | Use *77* as a CW-friendly sign-off.                                          |
|  *SK*   | Silent Key; a deceased radio amateur. End of contract                                    | Outside than end of contact, *SK* can refer to a radio amateur who has died. |
|  *EE*   | Friendly way to end a contact.                                                           | "dit dit" *EE* as a friendly way to end a contact.                           |

### Simple QSO flow
|CW|Meaning|Use it like this|
|:-:|:--|---|
|*QRL?*|Is the frequency busy?|Send *QRL?* before calling.|
|*CQ*|Calling any station.|Send *CQ* to look for a contact.|
|*DE*|This is.|Use *DE* before your call sign.|
|*K*|Go ahead.|End your call with *K*.|
|*RST*|Signal report.|Give the other station an *RST* report.|
|*OP*|Operator name.|Send *OP* followed by your name.|
|*QTH*|Location.|Send *QTH* followed by your location.|
|*RIG*|Radio.|Send *RIG* followed by your radio model.|
|*ANT*|Antenna.|Send *ANT* followed by your antenna type.|
|*PWR*|Power.|Send *PWR* followed by your power in watts.|
|*WX*|Weather.|Send *WX* followed by the weather.|
|*QSL*|Confirmed.|Send *QSL* when you copied the information.|
|*TNX*|Thanks.|Send *TNX* before closing.|
|*73*|Best regards.|Send *73* at the end.|
|*SK*|End of contact.|Send *SK* as the final end.|
